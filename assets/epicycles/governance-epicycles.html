The puzzle. Every governance failure of the last fifteen years is the same failure: we never defined the model. Each new regulation answers the question by writing a slightly different definition next to the last one — patching what the previous text awkwardly included or excluded — until the field is run on twelve overlapping vocabularies that almost, but never quite, line up.

The Ptolemy parallel. When ancient astronomers' simple deferent model failed to match observation, they did not redraw the diagram. They added a smaller circle on top. Then a smaller one on top of that. By Copernicus's day, more than eighty epicycles were stacked against the heavens. Each worked. Each was wrong. The system described was wrong; the patches were just clever enough to keep it running.

The five definitions. Stack them and watch the chaos grow. SR 11-7 (2011) anchors on quantitative method — Black-Scholes is in, generative output is out. The NAIC Bulletin (2023) anchors on machine-based system influencing decisions. The EU AI Act (2024) anchors on inference, then introduces a separate definition of "model" for general-purpose AI — same word, different referent. OSFI E-23 (effective 2027) anchors on processes inputs to results, sweeping AI/ML in by name. Five regulators, five anchor verbs, all chasing the same elephant from different angles.

The newest epicycle. SR 26-2 landed sixteen days before this talk. It tightens SR 11-7's net — adding the word complex so simple spreadsheets escape — and then explicitly states that generative AI and agentic AI "are novel and rapidly evolving" and so "are not within the scope of this guidance." Three new carveouts ride on the regulation's body like the smaller circles Ptolemy stacked on his largest deferent. The fix patches the symptom and lets the practitioner keep operating, while the central question is left unanswered for the third time in a generation.

The hidden geometry. Each definition works for its slice. That is why epicycles persist — they are not stupid, they are locally correct. The slider here adds them one at a time so the cost of the geometry becomes visible: by the time all eight are running, the orbits overlap, the labels collide, and the trace of "what counts as a model" is a smear. No single navigator could steer by it.

The redrawn diagram. The right panel is the same scene with a different center. A model is a versioned, typed operator with a formally separable runtime environment. Versioned: anything that can change the output has a number. Typed: inputs and outputs have shape and meaning. Operator: a function from inputs to outputs — not a notebook, not an API, not a vibe. Separable runtime: the model is not the GPU it ran on. Once the operator is defined, every regulator's controls map onto its components. The policy engine scales with risk tier, not with the buzzword. One inventory. One lifecycle. One controls map. The math does not care what year it is.
